## 1. Dormancy Triggers
An account status changes programmatically based on inactivity timelines. Inactivity is defined as the absence of any customer-initiated financial transactions (withdrawals, deposits, transfers).
* Active: 0 to 180 days of inactivity.
* Inactive: 181 to 730 days (24 months) of inactivity. Debit cards are temporarily suspended.
* Dormant: 731+ days of inactivity. The account is frozen. No outward transactions are permitted, and all linked digital access is revoked.

## 2. Reactivation Process
To reactivate a Dormant account, the customer must physically visit a branch.
* The branch agent must verify the customer's identity via biometric fingerprint scanning.
* The customer must submit a signed "Account Reactivation Request" form.
* The local system will trigger a mandatory refresh of the customer's KYC data before unfreezing the account ledger.
