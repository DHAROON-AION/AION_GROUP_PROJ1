## 1. Joint Account Mandate Types
When opening a joint account, the applicants must legally define the operational mandate, which dictates how the core system processes transaction requests.

### 1.1 "Either or Survivor" Mandate
* Signature Requirement: Any single account holder can authorize transactions, sign cheques, and withdraw funds independently.
* Digital Access: Both holders receive distinct mobile banking credentials. The AION assistant will process requests from either user's authenticated session equally.
* Survivorship: In the event of death of one holder, the account balance automatically vests to the surviving holder.

### 1.2 "Jointly" Mandate
* Signature Requirement: All financial transactions require the cryptographic or physical signature of all registered account holders.
* Digital Access: Digital transfers initiated by User A will remain in a "Pending Authorization" state until User B logs in and approves the transaction via their local app portal.
* Survivorship: In the event of death, the account is immediately frozen until a formal sharia court decree determines the distribution of the deceased's share.

## 2. Dispute Protocol
If an official dispute is logged between joint holders, the bank will immediately apply a system-level freeze on all outward transfers until a mutual written agreement is provided.
