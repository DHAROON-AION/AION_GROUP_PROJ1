## 1. Delinquency Timelines and Escalation
The automated collection engine triggers specific actions based on the number of Days Past Due (DPD).
* DPD 1 - 15 (Grace Period): Automated SMS reminders and in-app push notifications. No penal charges applied.
* DPD 16 - 30: Outbound call from the Tier-1 collections desk. Account flagged internally as "Past Due."
* DPD 31 - 90: Escalation to Tier-2 collections. Late payment fees applied. Core banking system blocks all outward transfers from the customer's current accounts to force settlement.
* DPD 91+: Account officially classified as "Non-Performing Loan" (NPL). Customer is reported to the National Credit Bureau (SIMAH) as a defaulter.

## 2. Restructuring and Hardship Protocol
* If a customer provides verifiable proof of unexpected job loss or medical emergency prior to DPD 90, the bank may offer a one-time restructuring plan.
* Restructuring options include granting a 6-month payment holiday or extending the loan tenure to reduce the EMI, subject to internal credit committee approval.
