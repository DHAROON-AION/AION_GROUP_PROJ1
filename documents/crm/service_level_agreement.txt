## 1. Customer Inquiry Resolution SLAs
The following Service Level Agreements (SLAs) dictate the maximum permissible time to resolve or escalate customer queries based on the channel and severity.

### 1.1 Automated Digital Channels
* Standard Balance/Transaction Query: < 2.0 seconds (Target local LLM serving response time)
* Document Retrieval (Statements, IBAN certificates): < 5.0 seconds
* System Availability Uptime: 99.99% (Measured monthly)

### 1.2 Human-Assisted Channels
* General Inquiry (Secure Message): 12 business hours
* Dispute/Fraud Claim Initial Triage: 1 business hour
* Loan Application Preliminary Decision: 24 business hours

## 2. Infrastructure & Data Handling SLAs
To maintain strict compliance with SAMA guidelines regarding data residency, the internal AI assistant and underlying microservices must adhere to the following operational constraints:
* Data Transit: Customer data must never leave the bank's own infrastructure. All retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) processes must occur within the secured local network.
* Database Latency: Semantic search queries executed against local Qdrant or pgvector instances must return context chunks within 500 milliseconds to prevent timeout cascades during chat sessions.
* Container Orchestration: All backend services facilitating customer interactions must be containerized via Docker for immediate failover, guaranteeing zero downtime during the current deployment sprint ending August 04, 2026.
